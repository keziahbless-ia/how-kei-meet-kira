There’s this girl named Akira—but honestly, that name barely contains who she really is. She’s the kind of person na parang quiet lang at first glance, pero once you get close, you realize she carries a whole universe inside her—soft thoughts, deep feelings, and a faith that hums quietly in the background of everything she does. She has this way of making people feel seen without trying too hard, like her presence alone is already a form of comfort. She laughs gently, cares intentionally, and moves through life with a kind of sincerity that’s rare these days. And somewhere between her prayers, her late-night conversations, and her unspoken kindness, you’ll find that she’s not just someone you meet—she’s someone you end up being grateful for, the kind you don’t easily forget.

Then one day, she met someone unexpected...his name is Keziah—and somehow, he didn’t arrive loudly, but he stayed deeply. He’s the kind of person who carries both creativity and conviction, someone who sees the world not just as it is, but as something he can shape, redesign, and give meaning to. May quiet intensity siya—yung tipong mapapaisip ka kung ano bang tumatakbo sa isip niya, only to realize later that he feels things deeply and expresses them in ways that linger. He listens, he observes, and when he cares, it shows—not in grand gestures, but in consistency and thoughtfulness. Between his words, his art, and his faith, you’ll see a man who’s still becoming, still growing, but already capable of leaving an impact—and maybe, just maybe, someone who became unexpectedly important in her story.

They met in Batis Aramin on Valentine's Day, at exactly 8:00 in the evening. The night felt still, almost as if it paused just enough to let that moment exist. Walang engrandeng eksena, walang dramatic na music dahil magkalaban sila sa Editorial Cartooning—just two people meeting at a place that would later feel more meaningful than it first seemed. And in that simple, quiet encounter, something began—something neither of them fully understood yet, but both would eventually feel.

Since that last time they saw each other, it never happened again—after all, one is from Batangas and the other from Laguna. But despite the distance, every day and every night, they kept talking. Even if they weren’t physically together, they could feel each other’s presence in the messages, in the simple “how are you?” and in the laughs and stories shared through the screen. It was as if, even apart, they were slowly building a world just for the two of them—a space where they could be real, share dreams, and savor every little moment, even if only virtually.
